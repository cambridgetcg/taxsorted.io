# Venture Capital Trust (VCT): Legislative Profile

> **Last Updated**: 2026-02-01
> **Confidence**: High
> **Status**: Complete

---

## Overview

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Legal Form** | Listed company (approved by HMRC) |
| **Separate Legal Personality** | Yes |
| **Tax Personality** | **Exempt** - no CT on gains or income |
| **Primary Tax** | Corporation Tax (but exempt) |
| **CGT Applicable** | Exempt |
| **IHT Applicable** | No (company); shares in investor's estate |
| **VAT Applicable** | Standard rules |
| **NI Applicable** | Employer NI |

---

## Key Principle

> A VCT invests in **small, high-risk companies** and provides tax incentives to retail investors. The VCT itself is tax-exempt; investors get income tax relief and tax-free dividends.

**Dual Benefit**:
1. VCT level: Exempt from CT on gains and income
2. Investor level: 30% income tax relief + tax-free dividends

## VCT Conditions (ITA 2007 Part 6 Chapter 4)

### Section 274: Overview

To qualify as a VCT:

| Condition | Section | Requirement |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| **Income** | s.274(2)(a) | ≥70% from shares/securities |
| **Qualifying holdings** | s.274(2)(b) | ≥80% in qualifying holdings |
| **Investment** | s.274(2)(c) | ≥70% of qualifying holdings invested |
| **Listing** | s.274(2)(d) | Shares listed on EU regulated market |
| **Retention** | s.274(2)(e) | Income fully distributed |
| **No substantial interest** | s.274(3) | ≤15% in any one company |

### Section 280: Qualifying Holding

Investment must be in qualifying company:

| Test | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **Size** | <250 employees, ≤£15m gross assets |
| **Age** | First commercial sale <7 years ago (or 10 for KIC) |
| **Trade** | Qualifying trade |
| **UK establishment** | Must have UK permanent establishment |
| **Risk to capital** | Genuine risk (from 2018) |

### Excluded Activities (s.303)

VCTs cannot invest in companies doing:

| Activity | Excluded? |
|----------|----------|
| Property development | Yes |
| Financial activities | Yes |
| Legal/accountancy | Yes |
| Farming | Yes |
| Hotels/care homes | Yes (from 2015) |
| Subsidised energy | Yes (from 2015) |

## VCT Tax Exemptions

### CTA 2010 Section 615: Income Exemption

```
A VCT is not liable to corporation tax on any income
it receives (whether from investments or otherwise).
```

### CTA 2010 Section 617: Gains Exemption

```
A VCT is not liable to corporation tax in respect of
any chargeable gain accruing to it.
```

### Effect

| Income/Gain | VCT Treatment |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Dividends received** | Exempt |
| **Interest** | Exempt |
| **Capital gains** | Exempt |
| **Trading profits** | Exempt (but VCTs shouldn't trade) |

## Investor Reliefs

### ITA 2007 Section 261: Income Tax Relief

**30% Relief** on new VCT shares (up to £200,000 per year):

```
INVESTOR RELIEF EXAMPLE

Investment: £100,000 in new VCT shares
Relief: 30% × £100,000 = £30,000

Net cost: £70,000
```

### Conditions for Relief

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **New shares** | Subscribed for (not bought in market) |
| **Holding period** | 5 years minimum |
| **Maximum** | £200,000 per year |

### Section 265: Dividend Relief

Dividends from VCT shares are **tax-free**:

```
Dividends received... are treated as not being income
of the investor for income tax purposes.
```

### TCGA 1992 Section 151A: CGT Relief

Gains on disposal of VCT shares are **exempt from CGT**:

```
A gain accruing to an individual on a disposal of
ordinary shares in a [VCT] is not a chargeable gain.
```

## Summary of Investor Benefits

| Benefit | Detail | Condition |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| **Income tax relief** | 30% of investment | New shares, hold 5 years |
| **Tax-free dividends** | No income tax | Any VCT shares |
| **CGT exemption** | No CGT on disposal | Any VCT shares |
| **Annual limit** | £200,000 | For income tax relief |

### Withdrawal of Relief

If shares sold within 5 years:
- Income tax relief clawed back
- Dividend exemption continues
- CGT exemption continues

## VCT Investment Strategy

### Typical Portfolio

```
VCT PORTFOLIO STRUCTURE

VCT (listed, HMRC approved)
├─ Qualifying Holdings (≥80%)
│   ├─ Tech startup 1: £2m
│   ├─ Tech startup 2: £1.5m
│   ├─ Healthcare company: £2m
│   └─ [etc.]
│
├─ Non-Qualifying (≤20%)
│   ├─ Cash
│   └─ Gilts/bonds
│
└─ Distributions to shareholders (tax-free)
```

### Knowledge-Intensive Companies (KIC)

Enhanced limits for KICs:

| Limit | Standard | KIC |
|-------|----------|-----|
| **Company age** | 7 years | 10 years |
| **Annual investment** | £5m | £10m |
| **Lifetime investment** | £12m | £20m |

## Comparison: VCT vs EIS

| Aspect | VCT | EIS |
|--------|-----|-----|
| **Structure** | Listed fund | Direct investment |
| **Income tax relief** | 30% | 30% |
| **CGT exemption (investment)** | Yes (always) | Yes (if held 3+ years) |
| **CGT deferral** | No | Yes |
| **Loss relief** | No | Yes |
| **Minimum hold** | 5 years | 3 years |
| **Annual limit** | £200,000 | £1m (£2m KIC) |
| **Dividends** | Tax-free | Normal dividend tax |
| **Liquidity** | Market (listed) | None |

## HMRC Approval Process

### Provisional Approval

Before launch:
- Submit business plan
- HMRC reviews qualifying conditions
- Provisional approval granted

### Full Approval

After investments made:
- Evidence of qualifying holdings
- HMRC confirms VCT status
- Annual monitoring

### Withdrawal of Approval

If conditions breached:
- Warning/correction period
- Approval withdrawn
- Tax exemptions lost
- Investor reliefs may be clawed back
