# Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT): Legislative Profile

> **Last Updated**: 2026-02-01
> **Confidence**: High
> **Status**: Complete

---

## Overview

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Legal Form** | UK resident company (close or listed) |
| **Separate Legal Personality** | Yes |
| **Tax Personality** | Two-tier: Exempt on property + Taxable on residual |
| **Primary Tax** | Corporation Tax (with ring-fenced exemption) |
| **CGT Applicable** | Exempt on property disposals (ring-fenced) |
| **IHT Applicable** | No (shares may be in shareholder's estate) |
| **VAT Applicable** | Standard rules |
| **NI Applicable** | Employer NI on employees |

---

## Key Principle

> A REIT is a **tax-transparent property investment vehicle**. The property rental business is **exempt from CT**, but distributions to shareholders are taxed as property income in shareholders' hands.

**Mechanism**: Tax exemption at company level + tax on distributions = effective pass-through.

## CTA 2010 Part 12: REITs

### Section 518: Basic Conditions

To qualify as a REIT:

```
(1) A company must—
    (a) be UK resident,
    (b) be a company limited by shares,
    (c) be listed on a recognised stock exchange, or
        [from 2012] be a close company meeting conditions.
```

### The Three Tests

| Test | Requirement | Section |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Property rental business** | ≥75% assets in qualifying property | s.531 |
| **Income test** | ≥75% profits from property rental | s.532 |
| **Distribution test** | Distribute ≥90% of exempt profits | s.530 |

---

## Ring-Fenced Exemption

### Section 534: Exempt Business

```
Profits of the property rental business are not chargeable
to corporation tax.
```

### What Qualifies

| Activity | Ring-Fenced (Exempt)? |
|----------|----------------------|
| **UK property rental** | Yes |
| **Overseas property rental** | Yes (if certain conditions) |
| **Property trading** | No (residual business) |
| **Development for sale** | No (residual) |
| **Interest income** | No (residual) |
| **Property gains** | Yes (if ring-fenced property) |

### Ring-Fence Calculation

```
REIT PROFITS STRUCTURE

Total Company Profits
├─ Ring-Fenced Business (Property Rental)
│   ├─ Rental income
│   ├─ Property gains
│   └─ EXEMPT from CT
│
└─ Residual Business
    ├─ Trading profits
    ├─ Interest income
    ├─ Other gains
    └─ TAXABLE at standard CT rates
```

---

## Distribution Requirement

### Section 530: 90% Distribution

```
A REIT must distribute at least 90% of the profits of
the property rental business within 12 months of the
accounting period end.
```

### Property Income Distribution (PID)

Distributions from ring-fenced profits:

| Aspect | Treatment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Company** | Deductible (not a dividend) |
| **UK individual shareholder** | Property income (taxed at income rates) |
| **UK corporate shareholder** | Property income (taxed at CT rate) |
| **Non-resident shareholder** | 20% withholding (treaties may reduce) |

### Non-PID Distributions

Distributions from residual profits:
- Taxed as ordinary dividends
- No withholding

---

## Conditions: Assets and Income

### Section 531: Asset Test

≥75% of company's assets must be property rental assets:

```
Property Rental Assets
──────────────────────── ≥ 75%
Total Assets
```

### Section 532: Income Test

≥75% of profits must be from property rental:

```
Property Rental Profits
────────────────────────── ≥ 75%
Total Profits
```

**Breach**: If tests fail, REIT status can be lost (with tax consequences).

## Close Company REITs

### From 2012: Close Companies Allowed

Originally REITs had to be listed. FA 2012 allowed close company REITs:

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Institutional investor** | Must have ≥1 institutional investor |
| **Shareholding** | No single holder >50% (some exceptions) |
| **Tax avoidance** | Must not be arrangement to avoid tax |

**Source**: CTA 2010 s.528A (inserted by FA 2012)

---

## Entry and Exit

### Entering REIT Regime

| Step | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | Meet all conditions |
| 2 | Notify HMRC (CTA 2010 s.523) |
| 3 | Entry charge may apply (on certain gains) |

### Exit Charge

If REIT status lost within 10 years:
- Tax on previously exempt gains
- Clawback of tax benefits

### Voluntary Exit

Company can elect to leave regime:
- Notify HMRC
- Exit charge applies

---

## Shareholder Taxation

### ITA 2007 Section 973: PID Treatment

PIDs taxed as property income in shareholder's hands:

| Shareholder | Tax Treatment |
|-------------|---------------|
| **Individual (basic rate)** | 20% |
| **Individual (higher rate)** | 40% |
| **Individual (additional)** | 45% |
| **Company** | CT rate (19-25%) |
| **Pension fund** | Exempt |
| **ISA** | Exempt |
| **Non-resident** | 20% withholding |

**Note**: NOT taxed as dividends (no dividend allowance applies).

## Common REIT Structures

### Single Company REIT

```
REIT (listed or close)
├─ Ring-fenced: UK rental portfolio
├─ Residual: Development, trading
└─ Distributes 90%+ of rental profits
```

### Group REIT

```
Parent REIT (listed)
├─ Subsidiary 1 (UK property)
├─ Subsidiary 2 (UK property)
└─ Subsidiary 3 (residual)

Group treated as single REIT
```

## Comparison: REIT vs Direct Investment

| Aspect | Direct Property | REIT Investment |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **CT on rental** | Taxable | Exempt |
| **CT on gains** | Taxable | Exempt (ring-fenced) |
| **Shareholder tax** | Dividend rates | Property income rates |
| **SDLT** | On purchase | No (shares) |
| **Liquidity** | Low | High (if listed) |
| **Minimum investment** | High | Low (share price) |
