## The Key Difference

### Partnership Act 1890, Section 4(2)

```
"In Scotland a firm is a legal person distinct from the
partners of whom it is composed..."
```

This is the **only** provision in UK law that gives partnerships separate legal personality.

**Implications**:

| Aspect | English Partnership | Scottish Partnership |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Legal Personality** | No | Yes |
| **Own Property** | No (held by partners) | Yes (firm owns) |
| **Contract** | Partners contract | Firm contracts |
| **Sue/Be Sued** | Partners sued | Firm sued |
| **Borrow** | In partners' names | In firm's name |
| **Insolvency** | Partners sequestrated | Firm + partners separately |
| **Be Member of Other Entity** | Partners join | Firm can join |

---

## Tax Treatment: Still Transparent

> **Critical Point**: Despite having separate legal personality, Scottish partnerships are **still tax-transparent**.

The tax rules in ITTOIA 2005 Part 9 apply regardless of jurisdiction. The partners are taxed on their shares of profit, NOT the partnership.

```
Scottish Partnership
├─ Legal entity: YES (can own property, contract, sue)
├─ Tax entity: NO (transparent - partners taxed)
└─ Same tax treatment as English partnership
```

## Scottish Limited Partnership (SLP)

### Unique Features

Scottish LPs combine:
- Separate legal personality (Partnership Act 1890 s.4(2))
- Limited partner protection (Limited Partnerships Act 1907)
- Tax transparency

**Popular for**: Fund structures, private equity, property investment

### Registration

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Registrar** | Companies House (Scotland) |
| **Formation** | LP1 form |
| **Annual Filing** | Confirmation statement |
| **Accounts** | Not publicly filed (unlike LLPs) |

### Regulatory Changes (Anti-Money Laundering)

Due to misuse:

| Date | Change | Source |
|------|--------|--------|
| 2017 | PSC register required | SI 2017/514 |
| 2017 | Annual confirmation statement | SI 2017/514 |
| 2018 | Stricter beneficial ownership | SI 2018/708 |

**Source**: The Scottish Partnerships (Register of People with Significant Control) Regulations 2017

## Why Scottish Partnerships Are Used

### Fund Structures

```
TYPICAL FUND STRUCTURE

Scottish LP (Fund Vehicle)
├─ General Partner: Ltd company (unlimited liability)
├─ Limited Partners: Investors (limited liability)
└─ Benefits:
   ├─ Separate legal personality (can own assets directly)
   ├─ Tax transparency (investors taxed, not fund)
   ├─ Limited liability for LPs
   └─ No public accounts filing
```

### Property Investment

- Partnership owns property directly (unlike English partnership)
- Land registration in partnership name
- Simpler conveyancing on partner changes

## Anti-Avoidance & Regulatory Scrutiny

Scottish partnerships (especially LPs) have faced scrutiny due to misuse:

| Issue | Response |
|-------|----------|
| **Money laundering** | PSC register required |
| **Tax avoidance** | Mixed membership rules (ITA 2007) |
| **Shell companies** | Enhanced due diligence |
| **Opaque ownership** | Beneficial ownership disclosure |

**Partnerships (Prosecution) (Scotland) Act 2013**: Allows prosecution of partnerships as entities.

---

## Insolvency: Unique Position

Because Scottish partnerships have legal personality:

| Process | English Partnership | Scottish Partnership |
|---------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Partnership Debt** | Partners personally liable | Firm primarily liable |
| **Insolvency** | Partners sequestrated | Firm sequestrated (then partners if needed) |
| **Court Action** | Against partners | Against firm |

**Source**: Insolvency Act 1986, as applied to Scotland
