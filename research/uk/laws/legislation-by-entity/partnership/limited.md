## Key Principle

> A Limited Partnership combines the tax transparency of a general partnership with **limited liability for limited partners** (but NOT the general partner).

**Two classes of partner**:
- **General Partner(s)**: Unlimited liability, manages the business
- **Limited Partner(s)**: Liability limited to contribution, CANNOT manage

## Limited Partnerships Act 1907

### Section 4: Definition and Registration

**Original (enacted 28 August 1907)**:
```
(1) Limited partnerships may be formed... consisting of one or
    more persons called general partners, who shall be liable
    for all debts and obligations of the firm, and one or more
    persons to be called limited partners...

(2) A limited partner shall at the time of entering into such
    partnership contribute thereto a sum or sums as capital or
    property valued at a stated amount, and shall not be liable
    for the debts or obligations of the firm beyond the amount
    so contributed.
```

**Note**: Substantially unchanged since 1907.

### Section 5: Registration Required

```
Every limited partnership must be registered as such in
accordance with the provisions of this Act.
```

**Registrar**: Companies House

### Section 6: Limited Partner Restrictions

```
(1) A limited partner shall not take part in the management
    of the partnership business...

    If a limited partner takes part in the management of the
    partnership business he shall be liable for all debts and
    obligations of the firm incurred while he so takes part
    as though he were a general partner.
```

**The Critical Rule**: Limited partners LOSE their limitation if they manage.

## Tax Treatment: Transparent

### Same as General Partnership

Per ITTOIA 2005 Part 9, LPs are transparent:
- Partnership calculates profits
- Profits allocated to partners per agreement
- Each partner taxed on their share

### Key Difference: Loss Restrictions

**ITA 2007 ss.103A-103D** (introduced by FA 2004, substantially amended FA 2014):

Limited partners can only claim sideways loss relief up to their **capital contribution**.

**Section 103B: The Cap**:
```
The amount of sideways relief or capital gains relief to which
the limited partner is entitled... is limited to the
limited partner's contribution to the firm.
```

**Definition of "contribution"** (s.103C):
- Capital contributed
- Plus: share of profits not drawn
- Less: amounts drawn out
- Less: liabilities taken on by firm

**History of Loss Restriction**:

| Date | Change | Source |
|------|--------|--------|
| 10 Feb 2004 | Initial restriction introduced | FA 2004 |
| 13 Mar 2007 | Extended to all "non-active" partners | FA 2007 s.26 |
| 6 Apr 2014 | Enhanced rules, "mixed membership" | FA 2014 |

## Scotland vs England & Wales

| Aspect | English LP | Scottish LP |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **Legal Personality** | No | Yes (Partnership Act 1890 s.4(2)) |
| **Own Property** | Partners hold | Firm holds |
| **Sue/Be Sued** | Partners | Firm |
| **PSC Register** | No | Yes (from 2017) |
| **Use Case** | Less common | Fund structures |

**Scottish LPs** are particularly popular because they combine:
- Separate legal personality (can own assets, contract)
- Tax transparency (pass-through)
- Limited liability for LPs

## Common Uses

### Fund Structures

```
TYPICAL PE/VC FUND STRUCTURE

Scottish LP (Fund)
├─ General Partner: GP Ltd (management company)
│   └─ Unlimited liability
│   └─ 2% carry typically
├─ Limited Partners: Institutional investors
│   └─ Limited to commitment
│   └─ 98% of capital
└─ Benefits:
    ├─ Tax transparency
    ├─ No entity-level tax
    ├─ LP liability protection
    └─ Regulatory familiarity
```

### Property Investment

- Asset held by partnership (Scotland) or partners (E&W)
- Rental profits flow to partners
- CGT on disposal at partner level

## Anti-Avoidance Specific to LPs

| Provision | Target | Source |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Loss Cap** | Losses > contribution | ITA 2007 s.103B |
| **Non-Active Partners** | Extended loss cap | ITA 2007 s.103D |
| **Mixed Membership** | Profit shifting | ITA 2007 ss.850A-C |
| **Film Schemes** | Tax shelter abuse | FA 2004-2007 |

### Film Partnership Schemes

LPs were historically used for film investment tax shelters. Multiple Finance Acts closed loopholes:

| Date | Scheme Target | Response |
|------|--------------|----------|
| 2004 | Sale and leaseback | FA 2004 s.118 |
| 2006 | Pre-production costs | FA 2006 |
| 2007 | Non-active partner rules | FA 2007 s.26 |
