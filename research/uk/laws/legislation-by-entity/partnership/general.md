# General Partnership (England & Wales): Legislative Profile

> **Last Updated**: 2026-02-01
> **Confidence**: High
> **Status**: Complete

---

## Overview

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Legal Form** | Unincorporated |
| **Separate Legal Personality** | **No** (England & Wales) |
| **Tax Personality** | Transparent - partners taxed |
| **Primary Tax** | Income Tax on individual partners |
| **CGT Applicable** | Yes (on partners' shares) |
| **IHT Applicable** | On partners' interests |
| **VAT Applicable** | Standard rules (partnership registers) |
| **NI Applicable** | Class 2 & 4 on individual partners |

---

## Key Principle

> A general partnership in England & Wales has **no separate legal personality**. The partnership is the partners collectively. Each partner is taxed on their share of profits.

**Contrast with Scotland**: Scottish partnerships DO have separate legal personality (Partnership Act 1890 s.4(2)).

## Partnership Act 1890

### Section 1: Definition of Partnership

**Original (enacted 14 August 1890)**:
```
Partnership is the relation which subsists between persons
carrying on a business in common with a view of profit.
```

**Note**: Unchanged since 1890.

### Section 4: Meaning of Firm

**Section 4(1)** (England & Wales):
```
Persons who have entered into partnership with one another are
for the purposes of this Act called collectively a firm, and
the name under which their business is carried on is called
the firm-name.
```

**Section 4(2)** (Scotland):
```
In Scotland a firm is a legal person distinct from the
partners of whom it is composed...
```

**Key Distinction**:
| Jurisdiction | Legal Personality |
|--------------|-------------------|
| England & Wales | NO |
| Scotland | YES |

**Source**: [Partnership Act 1890](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/Vict/53-54/39/contents)

## Tax Treatment

### ITTOIA 2005, Part 9: Partnerships

**Section 848: Application of Partnership Rules**:
```
This Part provides rules for calculating and charging to tax
income arising to partners in partnerships.
```

**Section 849**: Partnership profits calculated as if partnership were UK resident individual.

**Section 850**: Profits then allocated to partners according to partnership agreement.

### Transparency Mechanism

```
STEP 1: Calculate partnership profits (as if single person)
        └─ Apply ITTOIA 2005 Part 2 (trading income)
        └─ Deduct allowable expenses

STEP 2: Allocate profits to partners per agreement
        └─ If no agreement: equally

STEP 3: Each partner includes share in their Self Assessment
        └─ Individuals: SA104 (Partnership supplement)
        └─ Companies: Include in CT600

STEP 4: Each partner pays tax on their share
        └─ At their own marginal rates
```

**Source**: [ITTOIA 2005 Part 9](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2005/5/part/9)

## Partnership Returns

### TMA 1970, Section 12AA: Partnership Return

**Requirements**:
- "Nominated partner" responsible for filing SA800
- Return shows partnership income and allocation to partners
- Each partner then files personal return including their share

**Filing Deadlines**:
| Return | Deadline |
|--------|----------|
| **SA800 (Partnership)** | 31 October (paper) / 31 January (online) |
| **SA104 (Partner supplement)** | As part of partner's individual return |

**Source**: [TMA 1970 s.12AA](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1970/9/section/12AA)

## Partnership Profits Calculation

Same as sole trader under ITTOIA 2005 Part 2:

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| **Accounting Basis** | Cash basis (default from 2024) or accruals |
| **Deductible Expenses** | Wholly and exclusively for trade |
| **Capital Allowances** | Claimed by partnership |
| **Losses** | Allocated to partners, then individual relief |

### Individual Partners

| Tax | Treatment |
|-----|-----------|
| **Income Tax** | On allocated share (ITA 2007 rates) |
| **Class 2 NI** | Voluntary (from Apr 2024) |
| **Class 4 NI** | 6% / 2% on profits |
| **CGT** | On disposal of partnership share |

### Corporate Partners

| Tax | Treatment |
|-----|-----------|
| **Corporation Tax** | On allocated share (CTA 2009) |
| **No NI** | N/A for companies |

## Capital Gains (TCGA 1992)

### Section 59: Partnerships

**Key Rules**:
- Partnership assets treated as owned by partners in profit-sharing ratio
- Disposal of partnership share = disposal of share of each asset
- Revaluations may trigger gains
- Business Asset Disposal Relief may apply

**Sources**: [TCGA 1992 s.59](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1992/12/section/59)

---

## Changes in Partnership

### Partner Joining/Leaving

| Event | Tax Treatment |
|-------|---------------|
| **Partner joins** | Incoming partner acquires share of assets |
| **Partner leaves** | Outgoing partner disposes of share |
| **Goodwill** | May have CGT implications |
| **Profit share change** | May be disposal/acquisition |

### Basis Period Rules

From 2024-25: Tax year basis (aligned with tax year, not accounting period).

**Transition** (2023-24): "Overlap profits" relieved.
