## Key Principle

> A charitable company is a company (usually CLG) that is registered as a charity. It benefits from **extensive tax exemptions** but must apply income exclusively for charitable purposes.

**Dual Regulation**:
- Companies House (company law)
- Charity Commission (charity law)

## CTA 2010 Part 11: Charitable Companies

### Section 466: Overview

```
This Part provides for exemptions from corporation tax for
charities in respect of certain descriptions of income and gains.
```

### Section 478: Exemption for Charitable Trading Profits

**Text**:
```
A charity is exempt from corporation tax on profits of a trade
if the trade—
(a) is exercised in the course of carrying out a primary purpose
    of the charity, or
(b) the work in connection with the trade is mainly carried out
    by beneficiaries of the charity.
```

**Primary Purpose Trading**: Charity shop selling donated goods = exempt
**Beneficiary Work**: Disabled charity where beneficiaries make products = exempt

### Section 479: Exemption for Small Trading

```
A charity is exempt from corporation tax on profits of a trade
which is not within section 478 if the relevant condition is met.

The relevant condition is—
(a) if the turnover of the trade does not exceed the small
    trading limit, or
(b) if turnover exceeds the limit, the charity reasonably
    expected it not to exceed.
```

**Small Trading Limits**:

| Total Charity Income | Trading Limit |
|---------------------|---------------|
| Under £32,000 | £8,000 |
| £32,000 - £320,000 | 25% of income |
| Over £320,000 | £80,000 |

### Section 481: Non-Exempt Trading

If trading doesn't qualify under s.478 or s.479:
- Trading income is **taxable** at CT rates
- Solution: Transfer trading to separate trading subsidiary

### Section 486: Exemption for Property Income

```
A charity is exempt from corporation tax on profits arising
from any land or estate that it owns.
```

### Section 492: Exemption for Investment Income

```
A charity is exempt from corporation tax on—
(a) dividends and other distributions,
(b) non-trading credits in respect of loan relationships,
(c) non-trading gains on intangible fixed assets.
```

## The "Applicable" Condition

### Section 493: Income Must Be Applied for Charitable Purposes

**Critical Rule**:
```
The exemptions in this Part apply only so far as the income
or gain is applied to charitable purposes only.
```

If charity accumulates income without applying it, exemption can be lost.

**What "applied" means**:
- Spent on charitable activities
- Held for future charitable use (within reasonable time)
- Invested to generate income for charitable use

## Gift Aid (ITA 2007 ss.413-430)

### How It Works

```
GIFT AID MECHANISM

Donor gives £80 (net)
├─ Charity reclaims £20 (basic rate tax)
├─ Charity receives £100 gross
└─ If donor is higher rate:
    └─ Donor claims £20 relief on SA return
    └─ Total tax relief: £40 on £100 gift
```

### Section 416: Gift Aid Donations

```
A "gift aid donation" is a payment to a charity by an individual
which is not subject to any condition as to repayment.
```

### Corporate Gift Aid (CTA 2010 s.189-202)

Companies deduct charitable donations from total profits (not as expense).

## Trading Subsidiary Structure

### Why Use a Subsidiary?

Non-primary-purpose trading in the charity = taxable.

**Solution**:
```
TRADING SUBSIDIARY STRUCTURE

Charity (exempt)
    ↑ Gift Aid donation (100% of profits)
    │
Trading Subsidiary Ltd
├─ Conducts non-charitable trading
├─ Pays CT on profits (25%)
├─ BUT: Donates profits to parent charity
├─ Gift Aid deduction = no CT payable
└─ Net effect: Zero tax
```

### Legal Requirements

- Trading sub must be separate legal entity
- Charity must control trading sub
- Profit must be donated within 9 months of year end

## TCGA 1992 Section 256: CGT Exemption

```
A gain shall not be a chargeable gain if it accrues to a
charity and is applicable and applied for charitable purposes.
```

**Effect**: Charities pay no CGT on disposal of investments or property (if proceeds applied for charitable purposes).

## VAT Treatment

### Complex Rules

| Activity | VAT Treatment |
|----------|---------------|
| **Sales of donated goods** | Zero-rated |
| **Fundraising events** | Exempt (up to 15/year) |
| **Welfare services** | Exempt |
| **Educational** | Exempt |
| **Standard trading** | Standard rules apply |

### VAT Registration

Charities must register if taxable supplies exceed £90,000 threshold.

**Source**: VATA 1994 Schedule 8 (zero-rating), Schedule 9 (exemptions)
