## Key Principle

> A bare trust is **completely transparent** for tax purposes. The beneficiary is treated as owning the trust assets directly. The trustee is merely a nominee.

**Also Known As**:
- Simple trust
- Nominee arrangement
- Absolute trust (when beneficiary absolutely entitled)

## Definition: What Makes a Trust "Bare"?

### TCGA 1992 Section 60(1): The Nominee Rule

```
(1) In relation to assets held by a person as nominee for another
    person, or as trustee for another person absolutely entitled
    as against the trustee... this Act shall apply as if the
    property were vested in, and the acts of the nominee or
    trustee in relation to the assets were the acts of, the
    person or persons for whom he is the nominee or trustee.
```

### "Absolutely Entitled" Test

A beneficiary is absolutely entitled if they can:
1. Direct the trustee to transfer the asset to them, AND
2. Have no condition precedent to their entitlement

**Examples**:
| Situation | Bare Trust? |
|-----------|-------------|
| Adult beneficiary, unconditional | Yes |
| Minor beneficiary | No (cannot give receipt) |
| Beneficiary with life interest only | No |
| Nominee holding shares | Yes |

---

## Income Tax Treatment

### ITTOIA 2005 Section 649: Beneficiary's Income

When beneficiary is absolutely entitled, they are taxed directly:

```
The income is treated as the income of the beneficiary
(and not the trustee).
```

### Practical Effect

```
BARE TRUST INCOME FLOW

Trust receives £1,000 dividends
    │
    ↓ (No trust taxation)
    │
Beneficiary includes in their SA return
    │
    ↓
Taxed at beneficiary's marginal rates
```

**No Trustees' Rate**: Unlike discretionary trusts, there is NO 45% rate applied to income.

---

## Capital Gains Tax Treatment

### TCGA 1992 Section 60: Nominee Treatment

The trustee's acts are treated as the beneficiary's acts:

| Event | Who Reports Gain? | Rate |
|-------|-------------------|------|
| **Asset disposed** | Beneficiary | Beneficiary's CGT rate |
| **Asset transferred out** | No gain (same person) | N/A |

### Important Consequence

**No CGT on distribution**: Since beneficiary already "owns" the asset, transferring it out of bare trust is NOT a disposal.

Compare to discretionary trust: Distribution = disposal = CGT charge.

---

## IHT Treatment

### IHTA 1984 Section 49: Qualifying Interest in Possession

```
A person beneficially entitled to an interest in possession
in settled property shall be treated... as beneficially
entitled to the property in which the interest subsists.
```

### Effect on IHT

| Event | IHT Treatment |
|-------|---------------|
| **Creation** | PET (potentially exempt transfer) |
| **During trust** | In beneficiary's estate |
| **Beneficiary dies** | Charged as part of estate |
| **Distribution to beneficiary** | No charge (already "theirs") |

---

## Bare Trust vs Other Trusts

| Aspect | Bare Trust | Interest in Possession | Discretionary |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|---------------|
| **Income taxation** | Beneficiary | Life tenant | Trustees (45%) |
| **CGT rate** | Beneficiary's rate | Beneficiary's rate | 24% |
| **IHT treatment** | Beneficiary's estate | Life tenant's estate | Relevant property |
| **Transparency** | Full | Partial | None |

## Common Uses

### Nominee Arrangements

```
NOMINEE BARE TRUST

Beneficial Owner
    ↑
    │ (legal owner holds as nominee)
    │
Nominee (e.g., broker, solicitor)
    │
    │ Holds shares/property
    ↓
Underlying Asset
```

### Minor's Trust (Until 18)

If assets held for minor absolutely:
- Bare trust rules apply once they reach 18
- Until then: trustee reports income but may be taxed on child (parental settlement rules apply if from parent)

## Registration Requirements

### Trust Registration Service (TRS)

From 2022, **ALL UK express trusts** must register, including bare trusts - with some exceptions:

| Exception | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Will trusts (first 2 years)** | Exempt |
| **UK registered pension** | Exempt |
| **Life insurance** | Exempt |
| **Charitable trusts** | Exempt |

**Source**: Money Laundering Regulations 2017 (as amended 2022)

### SA Return

- Beneficiary reports income/gains on their personal return
- Trustee does NOT file SA900 (trust return) for bare trusts

---

## Parental Settlement Rules

### ITTOIA 2005 Section 629: Parent's Income

If parent settles assets on bare trust for own minor child:

```
Income arising from property... is treated for income tax
purposes as the income of the settlor (if living) and not
as the income of any other person.
```

**Exception**: If child's income from parent's settlement is ≤£100/year, taxed on child.

**Effect**: Parent taxed on income until child reaches 18.
