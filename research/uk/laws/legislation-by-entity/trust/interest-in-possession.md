## Key Principle

> An interest in possession (IIP) trust gives a beneficiary (the "life tenant") the **right to income** from the trust. Capital belongs to different beneficiaries (the "remaindermen").

**Key Terms**:
- **Life tenant**: Person entitled to income (usually for life)
- **Remaindermen**: Persons entitled to capital (usually on life tenant's death)
- **Interest in possession**: Current right to enjoy the trust income

## Income Tax Treatment

### Life Tenant Taxed on Income

Unlike discretionary trusts, the life tenant is taxed on the income:

```
IIP TRUST INCOME FLOW

Trust receives £10,000 rental income
    │
    ↓
Life tenant entitled to all income
    │
    ↓
Life tenant taxed at THEIR marginal rate
(not trust rates)
```

### ITTOIA 2005 Section 649: Beneficiary's Income

```
Income to which a beneficiary of a UK settlement is entitled
arising under the settlement... is treated as the income
of the beneficiary.
```

### Tax Rates Applied

| Life Tenant's Band | Rate on Trust Income |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| Basic rate | 20% (non-savings), 8.75% (dividends) |
| Higher rate | 40% / 33.75% |
| Additional rate | 45% / 39.35% |

**Contrast with Discretionary**: Discretionary always 45%/39.35%.

## CGT Treatment

### TCGA 1992 Section 72: Life Tenant's Death

When life tenant dies:

```
On the death of a person entitled to an interest in possession
in settled property, the trustees are deemed to have disposed
of and reacquired the settled property at market value.
```

**BUT**: Section 72(1)(b) provides that **no CGT** is charged on this deemed disposal.

### Uplift on Death

The trust assets get an uplift to market value (like assets owned personally):

| Event | CGT Consequence |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Life tenant dies** | Uplift to MV, no CGT |
| **Actual disposal by trustees** | CGT at trust rate (24%) |
| **Transfer to remainderman** | No gain/no loss |

## IHT Treatment: Critical Distinction

### Pre-22 March 2006 IIP Trusts

**IHTA 1984 Section 49 (old regime)**:

```
A person beneficially entitled to an interest in possession
in settled property shall be treated... as beneficially
entitled to the property in which the interest subsists.
```

**Effect**: Life tenant treated as OWNING the trust assets for IHT.

| Event | IHT Consequence |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Creation** | PET (potentially exempt) |
| **Life tenant dies** | In their estate (40%) |
| **Transfer to new life tenant** | PET |

### Post-22 March 2006 IIP Trusts

**FA 2006 Changes**: Most new IIP trusts are now "relevant property":

| Trust Type | IHT Treatment |
|------------|---------------|
| **Immediate post-death interest (IPDI)** | Section 49 treatment |
| **Disabled person's interest** | Section 49 treatment |
| **Transitional serial interest** | Section 49 treatment |
| **All others** | Relevant property (periodic/exit charges) |

---

## Types of IIP Trust (Post-2006)

### 1. Immediate Post-Death Interest (IPDI)

Created by will where beneficiary becomes entitled immediately on death:

```
IPDI EXAMPLE

Testator dies → Will trust created → Spouse gets life interest

This qualifies as IPDI:
- Spouse treated as owning assets (s.49)
- No periodic charges
- Spouse exemption may apply
```

### 2. Transitional Serial Interest (TSI)

Where interest passes from pre-2006 life tenant to new beneficiary under trust terms.

### 3. Disabled Person's Interest

Trust for disabled person meeting specific criteria (FA 2006 definitions).

### 4. Other IIP (Relevant Property)

Any IIP not falling into above categories:
- Subject to periodic charges (up to 6% every 10 years)
- Exit charges on distributions

| Aspect | Pre-22/3/2006 | IPDI/Disabled | Other Post-2006 |
|--------|--------------|---------------|-----------------|

| **IHT treatment** | In life tenant's estate | In life tenant's estate | Relevant property |

## Practical Example

### Traditional Will Trust

```
Husband dies 2010 (pre-FA 2006 rules don't apply - he died after)
BUT: This is an IPDI because created by will

STRUCTURE:
├─ Trust holds family home + £500,000 investments
├─ Widow has life interest (entitled to live in home, receive dividends)
├─ Children are remaindermen (get capital on widow's death)

TAX TREATMENT:
├─ Income: Widow taxed at her rates
├─ CGT: Uplift on widow's death
└─ IHT: In widow's estate (spouse exemption applies during marriage)

ON WIDOW'S DEATH:
├─ Trust assets valued
├─ Added to widow's estate
├─ Spouse exemption no longer available
└─ IHT potentially payable if combined estate > NRB
```

## Common Uses

### Will Trusts for Surviving Spouse

Most common use - protects family assets while providing for survivor:
- Survivor lives in house / receives income
- Children eventually inherit capital
- Protects against survivor remarrying and assets going to new family

### Protective Trusts

Life interest that converts to discretionary if beneficiary becomes bankrupt:
- Protects against creditors
- Section 33 Trustee Act 1925
