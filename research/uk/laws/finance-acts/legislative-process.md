## The Finance Act Annual Cycle

### Timeline: Budget to Royal Assent

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    ANNUAL FINANCE ACT CYCLE                                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

   AUTUMN/SPRING                                                    SPRING
   (Budget Day)                                                  (Royal Assent)
       │                                                              │
       ▼                                                              ▼
   ┌───────┐    ┌───────┐    ┌───────┐    ┌───────┐    ┌───────┐    ┌───────┐
   │Budget │───►│Budget │───►│Finance│───►│Commons│───►│Lords  │───►│Royal  │
   │Speech │    │Resol- │    │Bill   │    │Stages │    │Stages │    │Assent │
   │       │    │utions │    │Intro  │    │       │    │       │    │       │
   └───────┘    └───────┘    └───────┘    └───────┘    └───────┘    └───────┘
       │            │                          │
       │            │                          │
   Some taxes   Provisional         Committee → Report → 3rd Reading
   take effect  statutory effect

   ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
   Timeline:   Day 1      Days 1-4     ~Day 7      ~3-4 months   ~Day after
                                                                  Commons
```

### Stage 1: The Budget

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Budget Statement** | Chancellor presents tax and spending plans to House of Commons |
| **Budget Documentation** | Red Book, OOTLAR (Overview of Tax Legislation and Rates) |
| **Fiscal Event Types** | Spring Budget, Autumn Budget, Mini-Budget (rare) |
| **Timing** | Traditionally Autumn (November) or Spring (March) |

### Stage 2: Budget Resolutions (Ways and Means Resolutions)

Immediately after the Budget speech, the House of Commons votes on resolutions:

| Resolution Type | Purpose | Effect |
|-----------------|---------|--------|
| **PCTA 1968 Resolution** | Immediate tax changes | Legal effect from midnight on Budget day |
| **Ways and Means Resolutions** | Authorise Finance Bill provisions | Define scope of what can be in the Bill |

#### Provisional Collection of Taxes Act 1968

This is the mechanism for **immediate** tax changes:

```
PCTA 1968 - KEY CONDITIONS

1. Resolution must declare "expedient in the public interest"
2. Finance Bill must have 2nd reading within 30 SITTING DAYS
3. Finance Act must be enacted within 7 MONTHS
4. Expires on:
   - Dissolution of Parliament
   - Passage of the Act
   - Rejection of relevant provisions
```

**Example (Budget 2025)**: Immediate changes to:
- Stamp Duty Reserve Tax (SDRT) rates
- Tobacco duty rates

### Stage 3: Finance Bill Introduction

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Drafting** | Parliamentary Counsel + HMRC + Treasury |
| **Publication** | Typically 7-10 days after Budget |
| **First Reading** | Formal introduction (no debate) |
| **Second Reading** | Main debate on Bill's principles |

### Stage 4: House of Commons Stages

```
HOUSE OF COMMONS PROCEDURE

Second Reading
     │
     ▼
Committee Stage (SPLIT)
├── Committee of Whole House (key clauses)
└── Public Bill Committee (other clauses)
     │
     ▼
Report Stage (amendments debated)
     │
     ▼
Third Reading (final approval)
```

**Amendment Rules**:
- Must not exceed Budget resolution scope
- Cannot INCREASE tax beyond Government proposal
- Cannot introduce NEW taxes not in resolutions

### Stage 5: House of Lords

The Lords has **no power to amend** Finance Bills due to "financial privilege":

| Stage | What Happens |
|-------|--------------|
| **First Reading** | Formal introduction |
| **Second Reading** | Debate only (cannot block) |
| **Committee Stage** | "Negatived" - does not occur |
| **Report & Third Reading** | Formality - no amendments possible |

> **Constitutional basis**: Parliament Act 1911 - Money Bills can pass without Lords consent

### Stage 6: Royal Assent

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Timing** | Usually same day as Lords passage or next day |
| **Declaration** | "Le Roy remercie ses bons sujets, accepte leur benevolence, et ainsi le veult" |
| **Effect** | Bill becomes Finance Act [Year] |

## How Regulations Are Modified

### 1. Direct Amendment (Finance Act)

The Finance Act directly amends existing legislation:

```
EXAMPLE: Corporation Tax Rate Change

Finance Act 2023, Section X:
"In CTA 2010, Section 3, for '19%' substitute '25%'"

This DIRECTLY AMENDS the Corporation Tax Act 2010
```

### 2. Commencement Dates

Tax changes can have different effective dates:

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| **Immediate** | PCTA resolution - midnight on Budget day |
| **Start of tax year** | "From 6 April 2025" |
| **Retrospective** | "With effect from 1 January 2025" |
| **Future** | "From 1 April 2026" |
| **Appointed day** | "On a day appointed by Treasury order" |

### 3. Secondary Legislation (Statutory Instruments)

Finance Acts often delegate power to Treasury/HMRC to make detailed regulations:

```
ENABLING PROVISION (in Finance Act):
"The Treasury may by regulations make provision for..."

IMPLEMENTING SI (later):
"The [Name] Regulations 2025"
SI 2025/XXXX
```

| SI Type | Procedure | Parliament's Role |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Affirmative** | Requires approval vote | Debated & voted |
| **Negative** | Becomes law unless objected | Can be "prayed against" |
| **Commons-only** | Only needs Commons approval | Tax/financial matters |

**Volume**: ~3,500 SIs made per year; ~1,000 require Parliamentary consideration

## Types of Tax Change Mechanisms

### 1. Finance Act Provisions

| Change Type | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| **Rate changes** | Corporation tax 19% → 25% |
| **Threshold changes** | VAT registration £85k → £90k |
| **New reliefs** | SEIS introduced (Finance Act 2012) |
| **Abolition** | Non-dom regime abolished (Finance Act 2025) |
| **Anti-avoidance** | New targeted rules (TAARs) |
| **Structural changes** | MTD framework (Finance Act 2016) |

### 2. Statutory Instruments

| Change Type | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| **Annual uprating** | Van benefit charge indexed to CPI |
| **Technical implementation** | MTD regulations |
| **Appointed day orders** | Bringing provisions into force |
| **Rate setting** | Tax credit rates |

### 3. HMRC Guidance (NOT Law)

| Type | Status | Reliance |
|------|--------|----------|
| **HMRC Manuals** | HMRC's interpretation | Not binding; can be challenged |
| **Statements of Practice** | Explains HMRC's view where statute unclear | Higher status; legitimate expectation possible |
| **Extra-Statutory Concessions** | HMRC practice beyond strict law | Being phased out (put into legislation) |
| **Guidelines for Compliance** | HMRC's expectations | Reduces enquiry risk but not protection |

> **Key Legal Principle**: HMRC guidance is NOT law. "You shouldn't assume that the guidance is comprehensive or that it will provide a definitive answer" - HMRC

## Real-World Example: Finance Act 2025

### Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| **30 October 2024** | Autumn Budget announced |
| **30 October 2024** | PCTA resolution (immediate SDRT/tobacco changes) |
| **7 November 2024** | Finance Bill published |
| **November 2024** | Second Reading |
| **Jan-Feb 2025** | Committee stages |
| **3 March 2025** | Report Stage (66 Government amendments) |
| **4 March 2025** | Lords First Reading |
| **19 March 2025** | Lords passage |
| **20 March 2025** | Royal Assent → Finance Act 2025 |

### Key Changes Enacted

| Measure | Effective Date | Mechanism |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| CGT rate changes | Budget day (PCTA) | Immediate |
| Non-dom abolition | 6 April 2025 | Start of tax year |
| SDLT rate changes | 1 April 2025 | Specified date |
| Private school VAT | 1 January 2025 | Specified date |
| Corp tax rate 25% | Already in force | Previous FA |

## Interaction: Finance Acts vs Existing Legislation

### Consolidated Acts Modified by Finance Acts

| Consolidated Act | Covers | Modified By |
|------------------|--------|-------------|
| **Income Tax Act 2007** | Income tax rules | Annual Finance Acts |
| **Corporation Tax Act 2009** | CT trading income | Annual Finance Acts |
| **Corporation Tax Act 2010** | CT rates, reliefs | Annual Finance Acts |
| **TCGA 1992** | Capital gains | Annual Finance Acts |
| **VATA 1994** | VAT | Annual Finance Acts |
| **IHTA 1984** | Inheritance tax | Annual Finance Acts |

### How Amendments Work

```
ORIGINAL (CTA 2010, s.3):
"The main rate of corporation tax is 19%"

AMENDMENT (Finance Act 2023):
"In section 3 of CTA 2010, for '19%' substitute '25%'"

RESULT (CTA 2010, s.3 as amended):
"The main rate of corporation tax is 25%"
```

## The Multiple Finance Acts Phenomenon

In some years, multiple Finance Acts are passed:

| Scenario | Result |
|----------|--------|
| **Spring + Autumn Budgets** | Finance Act (No. 1) and Finance Act (No. 2) |
| **Early election** | Truncated Bill → smaller Act + new Bill |
| **Mini-Budget** | Additional Finance Act |

**Example 2024-25**: Finance (No. 2) Bill 2024-26 (following election and autumn budget)

## Practical Implications for TaxSorted

### Tracking Requirements

| What to Track | Why | Update Frequency |
|---------------|-----|------------------|
| **Budget announcements** | Early warning of changes | Event-driven |
| **PCTA resolutions** | Immediate effect changes | Budget day |
| **Finance Bill progress** | Amendments may alter provisions | Weekly during passage |
| **Royal Assent** | Final law | When enacted |
| **Statutory Instruments** | Detailed implementation | Ongoing |
| **HMRC Manuals** | Practical interpretation | Ongoing |

### Change Categories

```
IMPACT ASSESSMENT FOR TAXSORTED

HIGH IMPACT (require immediate app updates):
├── Rate changes (income tax bands, CGT rates)
├── Threshold changes (VAT registration, allowances)
├── New filing requirements (MTD phases)
└── Deadline changes

MEDIUM IMPACT (require content updates):
├── Relief changes (pension limits, EIS rules)
├── Anti-avoidance provisions
└── New exemptions

LOW IMPACT (monitoring only):
├── Technical clarifications
├── HMRC manual updates
└── Minor SIs
```
