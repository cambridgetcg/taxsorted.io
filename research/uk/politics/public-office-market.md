# UK public-office pathfinder — market and first-release decision

> **Section:** `research/uk/politics/`
> **Last updated:** 2026-07-15
> **Confidence:** High for the linked first-party descriptions; medium for whole-market completeness
> **Status:** Complete for the first-release decision

## Decision — TaxSorted inference

TaxSorted should build the missing connective layer between interest in public
office and the official steps for standing. It should not become a campaign
agency, a political party school or another directory of current candidates.

The first release should map two paths only:

1. Member of the UK Parliament at a general election in Great Britain; and
2. councillor for an English district, borough, county or unitary authority.

The product should help a person compare those offices, understand whether a
route may be open to them, see the party and independent branches, and leave at
the right official door with a sourced checklist. It must not decide legal
eligibility, recommend a party, predict election results, submit nomination
papers or act as an election agent.

Everything below is split into two kinds of statement:

- **Direct evidence** reports what a linked provider or official source says or
  visibly offers. A missing public price means only that no price was found on
  the pages reviewed; it does not prove that no price exists.
- **TaxSorted inference** is our market grouping, product judgement or release
  decision. It is not a claim made by the providers.

## Scan boundary

This scan checked public UK-facing web pages on 15 July 2026. It covers products,
guides and programmes for becoming a councillor, MP, devolved legislator, mayor,
Police and Crime Commissioner, political candidate, campaign agent or active
civic participant.

It does not claim to cover private party intranets, every local programme,
individual political consultants or courses sold only through private contact.
Prices and cohort availability can change. Product pages are first-party sources;
their impact claims are self-reports unless an independent source is named.

## Market categories — TaxSorted inference

The services group into six different jobs. The category names below are ours;
the providers do not use one shared market definition.

| Category | Main examples | What the user receives | What is usually missing |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Official rule and office explanation | Electoral Commission, UK Parliament, Scottish Parliament, Senedd | Authoritative role, eligibility, nomination, spending and process information | One route across institutions, personal sequencing and preparation for party selection |
| Councillor orientation | LGA Be a Councillor and its political-group support | Role explanation, stories, first steps, events and some candidate training | Cross-office comparison and one reusable path for every jurisdiction |
| Representation pipeline | Elect Her, Operation Black Vote, Patchwork Foundation | Confidence, networks, mentors, cohorts, lived experience and sometimes grants | Universal access and instant self-service at national scale |
| Commercial and partisan campaign craft | GetElected, ALDC, Labour Activate and other party schools | Campaign technique, templates, expert help and agent practice | A neutral, universally accessible public route; complete official provenance varies |
| Election and accountability infrastructure | Democracy Club and mySociety | Candidate lookup, representative contact, records, alerts and open data | The journey into candidacy and the work before nomination |
| Civic learning and organising | The Politics Project, Shout Out UK, Citizens UK and legislature petition tools | Political literacy, contact with representatives, issue organising and specific civic actions | Office-specific selection, nomination and candidate-compliance steps |

Public-facing official services and most charitable programmes are free to the
person using them. Visible revenue is concentrated in public or charitable
funding, school subscriptions, organisational training, party membership and
events, public-service contracts, APIs and quote-based campaign support. No
clear mass-market consumer subscription leader was found.

## Closest competitors — TaxSorted inference

“Competitor” here includes a free substitute for the user's attention. It does
not imply that an official body or charity shares TaxSorted's commercial aims.

| Service | Closest on | What it already does well | Space left for TaxSorted |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| LGA Be a Councillor | Official-style councillor discovery | Plain role explanation, party and independent routes, stories, events and recognised public backing | Compare council office with Parliament, turn a person's answers into a sourced sequence, and keep legal hand-offs in one place |
| Elect Her | End-to-end human journey | The strongest observed sequence from curiosity through standing to serving, with self-led and human support | A universal, no-cohort path that works for people outside its audience while complementing, not replacing, its specialist support |
| GetElected | Direct product and commercial model | Self-paced candidate and campaign-manager learning joined to expert services | A complete, source-led, non-partisan path with a clear free public-law boundary and no pressure to buy campaign services |
| Electoral Commission | Compliance substitute and required source of truth | Definitive election-specific rules, forms and candidate-agent stages | Explain which guide matters, when it matters and what the person should understand before entering the legal manual |
| Party campaign schools | Practical campaigning and agent craft | Real selection context, local knowledge, templates, organiser practice and peer networks | Neutral orientation before party choice and clear labels where a party alone controls the next step |

Democracy Club and mySociety are important neighbours rather than direct
pathfinder competitors. Their tools become useful when a person wants to inspect
an election, candidate, representative or public record. TaxSorted should link
to them instead of rebuilding those functions.

## White space — TaxSorted inference

The white space is not “more political information”. It is a reliable passage
through information that already exists in separate places.

A useful pathfinder should:

1. ask what kind of public work the person is considering, without asking how
   they vote;
2. compare the office's real work, level of power, electorate, likely route,
   term, time demands and publicly evidenced pay or allowances;
3. show eligibility as **likely**, **unlikely** or **needs an official check**,
   never as TaxSorted legal approval;
4. separate party selection, independent candidacy and election-law compliance;
5. build an ordered, dated checklist from the relevant election family;
6. link each legal step to the exact current official source;
7. name costs, safety concerns, uncertainty and human-controlled gates early;
8. end with a live next action, not a dead “coming soon” door; and
9. let the person leave with the path without creating an account or a political
   profile.

This is a navigation and explanation product. It can later save a private
checklist or remind a person of dates only after a separate privacy and consent
decision. It should not make the free official route harder in order to sell
those conveniences.

## Why the first release is MP in Great Britain plus an English principal councillor

### Direct facts supporting the boundary

- The Electoral Commission publishes one web guide for a UK Parliamentary
  general election in Great Britain and a different guide for Northern Ireland.
  Northern Ireland also has a separate Electoral Office hand-off.
- The Commission's English local-government guide expressly covers district,
  borough, county and unitary authority elections. It puts parish, mayoral,
  Greater London and other contests in separate guide families.
- UK Parliament already supplies the compact official MP introduction and the
  Commission supplies the detailed candidate-and-agent stages.
- The LGA supplies a public, government-funded role-and-first-steps layer for
  councillors and explicitly links Wales and Scotland to separate services.

### TaxSorted reasons

1. **Two genuinely different offices test the product.** An MP path tests a
   national legislature and a large electorate. A principal-councillor path
   tests local representation and a more common first elected office. If one
   path shape cannot explain both without hiding differences, the model needs
   fixing before expansion.
2. **The official source chain is unusually clear.** Parliament or the LGA can
   introduce the office; the Electoral Commission can anchor the legal stages;
   local returning officers or parties can own the steps that only they control.
3. **The jurisdiction boundary is honest.** Great Britain is the Commission's
   own UK Parliamentary guide boundary. England is the boundary of its principal
   local-government guide. TaxSorted does not invent “UK-wide councillor rules”.
4. **The release is narrow enough to verify.** Two paths are enough to test role
   comparison, eligibility questions, party and independent branches, source
   freshness and official hand-offs without maintaining every UK election law
   at once.
5. **It tests the market gap directly.** LGA is strong on councillor orientation;
   Parliament is brief on becoming an MP; both hand legal detail elsewhere. A
   shared pathfinder can prove whether joining those pieces is useful before
   adding campaign tools or human support.

For this release, “English principal councillor” means a candidate for a
district, borough, county or unitary authority election covered by the linked
Electoral Commission guide. It does not silently include a parish councillor,
elected mayor, Greater London Authority member or Police and Crime Commissioner.

The MP path is anchored to Great Britain general-election guidance. A live
by-election timetable and any election-specific variation remain an official
handoff until separately researched and tested.

### Deliberate later paths

| Later path | Why it is not silently included now |
| --- | --- |
| MP in Northern Ireland | Separate Electoral Commission guidance and Electoral Office for Northern Ireland hand-offs need their own source adapter and review. |
| Councillor in Scotland, Wales or Northern Ireland | Each nation has its own law, electoral system, terminology and official guidance. |
| Parish or community councillor | Separate election family and a different office context; “local councillor” must not blur the distinction. |
| MSP, Member of the Senedd or MLA | Different legislatures, voting systems, candidate routes and post-election duties. |
| Mayor, Greater London Authority member or Police and Crime Commissioner | Multiple election families and role variants, including combined roles, deposits and special disqualifications. |
| Election agent or campaign manager as the primary user | The Commission explains legal duties and parties teach practice, but a neutral training product needs a separate curriculum, risk review and validation. |

## Product guardrails — TaxSorted inference

### Truth and jurisdiction

- Put the office, election type, territory and law-as-at date beside every path.
- Use the relevant official guide as the legal spine. Paraphrase and link; do not
  copy a whole official guide into TaxSorted.
- Keep current law, a scheduled change, party practice and TaxSorted advice as
  four different things.
- Show the exact source and last check for each consequential eligibility,
  nomination, spending, deposit or deadline statement.
- If sources conflict or an edge case is unresolved, say “needs an official
  check” and give the correct contact route.
- Do not describe the scan or a path as complete merely because all known fields
  are filled.

### Political neutrality and privacy

- Never recommend a political party, candidate or ideology and never estimate
  how a person is likely to vote.
- A party-versus-independent question is a route choice, not a political-profile
  field.
- Do not collect political opinions, inferred beliefs, protected traits, private
  social accounts, home addresses or family details for the public path.
- Keep the first path usable without an account. If saved paths are introduced,
  make consent explicit, collect the minimum, state retention and provide a real
  delete control.
- Do not rank a person's suitability, popularity or chance of winning. Explain
  formal gates and practical commitments instead.

### Safety and authority

- State plainly that TaxSorted is not the Electoral Commission, Parliament, a
  returning officer, a political party, a lawyer or an election agent.
- Do not issue an eligibility certificate, accept a nomination, handle campaign
  money or claim that a form has been submitted.
- Put official harassment, intimidation, accessibility and candidate-safety
  routes beside the relevant stage; do not present abuse as a normal price of
  participation.
- Keep legal compliance separate from campaign tactics. A lawful step is not a
  promise of selection or election.
- Do not send a person to a party application, cohort or paid service without
  naming who controls it, whether access is restricted and any public cost.

### Product and money

- The sourced office map, rule explanation and official links belong in the
  free public corpus and should require no account.
- Any later paid feature must save real time or provide a separate human service;
  it must not conceal a required official step or create urgency around fear.
- Link to strong specialist services, especially representation programmes,
  instead of flattening their lived support into generic TaxSorted content.
- Check every outward door before release and at a bounded interval afterwards.
  When a door closes, label or remove it without erasing the source history.
- Publish a correction route and keep material source changes visible.

## First-release boundary — TaxSorted inference

The first release may contain:

- a plain comparison of the two offices;
- a territory and election-family gate;
- sourced eligibility questions with uncertain outcomes allowed;
- party and independent branches where both exist;
- a staged checklist from exploration to the official nomination hand-off;
- source dates, costs found in official material and warnings where a live
  election timetable is required;
- links to official guidance and suitable specialist support; and
- an export or print view that does not require an account.

It should not yet contain:

- nomination submission, campaign-finance returns or election-agent services;
- candidate, party or constituency recommendations;
- win-probability, voter-targeting or persuasion tools;
- a directory copied from Democracy Club or mySociety;
- live party-selection rules presented as law;
- parliamentary by-election, parish, mayoral, Police and Crime Commissioner,
  devolved-legislature or non-English council paths; or
- saved political profiles, reminders or personalised campaign services.

## Questions to answer before expansion

- Which Electoral Commission words and forms may be reproduced, and which
  should remain link-only under their current reuse terms?
- Which eligibility edge cases need a returning officer or specialist legal
  hand-off rather than another question in the path?
- How will party-controlled selection links be checked without implying that
  every party offers the same route or that TaxSorted endorses one?
- What evidence from real prospective candidates would show that the joined
  path saves confusion rather than merely adding another page?
- Which safety and accessibility needs should block release until the path
  handles them properly?
- What source-change monitor is proportionate before adding live election dates?
