## Shared promotion conditions

A candidate may move into a future corpus only after all of these are complete:

1. An appropriately qualified, independent UK reviewer checks the current legislation,
   territory, route, clock and adverse authorities on a recorded review date.
2. Each legal and procedural statement resolves to a current official or primary source.
   HMRC guidance is identified as HMRC's view where legislation or a binding judgment
   could control instead.
3. The record states the exact claimant and agent authority, competence boundary,
   conditionally regulated activity and jurisdiction-specific reserved work. A title or
   register entry is not treated as proof of competence.
4. The record separates the amount affected, qualifying base, amount claimed, amount
   accepted, amount received or credited, tax paid, interest, costs, professional fees
   and net client position. It predicts none of them.
5. The record includes a meaningful zero or adverse scenario, counterevidence and every
   route that must keep its own clock.
6. Privacy, security and threat review confirms that the public record needs no intake,
   upload, matching, filing, payment or identifiable evidence.
7. Tests prove fail-closed publication and source integrity. A new, separate publication
   decision must bind the resulting corpus version, digest and complete ID set.

Research may also end with `do-not-promote` where the route is too fact-dependent,
unsafe to express publicly, unsupported by current primary material or insufficiently
distinct from an existing record.

## Inheritance Tax: loss on sale after a death

**Status:** `candidate-question`

### Territory and route questions

- Is this one UK-wide Inheritance Tax record with jurisdiction-specific estate-authority
  gates, or do England and Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland require separate records?
- Which interests in land, shares, securities and collective investments fall within the
  current statutory routes?
- Which disposals must be included together, including disposals that moved in the other
  direction?
- How do purchases, exchanges, changes in holdings, compulsory acquisition and property
  already covered by another relief affect the calculation?

### Clock questions

- What event starts each disposal window and each claim window under the legislation in
  force on the review date?
- Do land and investment routes use different windows, extensions or fourth-period rules?
- When may an early claim remain provisional, and what later event makes it final?
- What separate estate-administration, payment, amendment or appeal clocks must continue
  while the claim is considered?

### Authority and professional-gate questions

- Who is the statutory “appropriate person” for each asset and who must sign?
- What evidence proves that a personal representative, trustee, transferee or beneficiary
  is both authorised and liable for the relevant tax?
- Which tasks require a jurisdiction-appropriate solicitor or other authorised legal
  professional, and which are tax computation or valuation work?
- When is an independent land, securities or business valuation needed, and which claimed
  professional status must be checked on a live register?

### Money, zero and adverse questions

- Which Inheritance Tax value may change if every condition is proved, and how does that
  interact with the asset's Capital Gains Tax acquisition value?
- When would there be no tax attributable to the asset and therefore no usable relief?
- Could aggregation, a later sale, a purchase, another relief, valuation evidence,
  interest or professional cost leave the client unchanged or worse off?

### Official and primary entry points

- [Inheritance Tax Act 1984](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1984/51/contents)
- [HMRC Inheritance Tax Manual: loss on sale of land](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/inheritance-tax-manual/ihtm33000)
- [HMRC Inheritance Tax Manual: loss on sale of shares](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/inheritance-tax-manual/ihtm34000)
- [HMRC form IHT35 entry point](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/inheritance-tax-claim-for-relief-loss-on-sale-of-shares-iht35)
- [HMRC form IHT38 entry point](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/inheritance-tax-claim-for-relief-loss-on-sale-of-land-iht38)

**Candidate-specific promotion condition:** a reviewer must reconcile the statute, both
HMRC manual routes, current forms, estate authority and Capital Gains Tax interaction
without turning a fall in value into an outcome prediction.

## Pension annual allowance and Scheme Pays

**Status:** `candidate-question`

### Territory and route questions

- Is the tax route substantively UK-wide, and which scheme or public-service rules require
  separate branches?
- How must pension input amounts from every relevant defined-contribution and
  defined-benefit arrangement be reconciled?
- Which standard, tapered, alternative and money-purchase allowance rules apply to the
  person and tax year under review?
- When is statutory Scheme Pays available, when is an arrangement only voluntary, and
  who remains liable in each case?

### Clock questions

- Which earlier tax years can contribute unused allowance on the review date, in what
  statutory order, and when does an unused amount cease to be available?
- What are the separate clocks for a pension savings statement, Self Assessment,
  correction, an original Scheme Pays notice and an amended notice?
- Which benefit-entitlement event can bring a notice clock forward, and which later
  information can extend it?

### Authority and professional-gate questions

- What written authority permits a professional to obtain and reconcile information from
  every scheme?
- What pension-tax, payroll, actuarial or accounting competence is needed for the actual
  arrangement?
- At what exact point would a personal recommendation, pension transfer, conversion or
  opt-out become an FCA-regulated activity?
- Which FCA permission and individual competence must be verified if that boundary is
  crossed?

### Money, zero and adverse questions

- Which tax-charge and pension-benefit states must be shown separately?
- How should a scheme debit, scheme interest or other adjustment be measured without
  presenting present cash flow as a gain?
- When could tapering, the money-purchase rules, missing statements, a failed statutory
  condition, a voluntary payment or later corrected information leave the person with
  more tax, less pension or both?

approve a branch-complete model of the tax charge, member liability and benefit debit.

## Double-tax treaty Mutual Agreement Procedure

**Status:** `candidate-question`

### Territory and route questions

- Which UK tax, person, treaty partner, treaty article and chargeable period are in scope?
- Does the exact treaty permit a Mutual Agreement Procedure request for the identified
  issue, and to which competent authority may the person present it?
- When is a protective presentation competent, what minimum information does it need,
  and what fuller evidence is later required?
- Which domestic appeal, payment, postponement or foreign process must remain separate
  from the government-to-government procedure?

### Clock questions

- What does the current UK legislation say, what does the exact treaty say, and which
  provision supplies the applicable presentation limit?
- What event is the first notification for this treaty and fact pattern?
- Could a foreign authority calculate the start or end differently?
- What later clock applies to any consequential domestic claim after a notified solution?

### Authority and professional-gate questions

- What signed authority must accompany an agent's presentation?
- Does the matter require transfer-pricing, permanent-establishment, residence,
  attribution or another treaty specialism?
- Which foreign adviser must confirm local procedure and the effect of any settlement,
  appeal or judgment?
- When would tribunal or court representation require a separately authorised lawyer?

### Money, zero and adverse questions

- Which two tax computations and currencies must be reconciled before and after each
  proposed adjustment?
- What interest, penalty, payment, foreign-exchange and consequential-relief states remain
  outside or alongside the treaty issue?
- Could either authority find the taxation treaty-compliant, decline to engage, be unable
  to depart from a domestic result or fail to agree?
- Could the person reject a proposed solution, or could time and professional cost produce
  no usable change?

### Official and primary entry points

- [Taxation (International and Other Provisions) Act 2010](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2010/8/contents)
- [UK tax treaties collection](https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/tax-treaties)
- [HMRC International Manual: Mutual Agreement Procedure](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/international-manual/intm423000)
- [HMRC Statement of Practice 1 (2018)](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/statement-of-practice-1-2018/statement-of-practice-1-2018)
- [HMRC guidance for companies considering MAP](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/double-taxation-objecting-if-your-company-isnt-being-taxed-correctly)

**Candidate-specific promotion condition:** one current treaty and one bounded issue must
be used to prove the model. No generic treaty clock or suggestion that the process must
produce agreement may enter the corpus.

## Negligible-value and share-loss relief

**Status:** `candidate-question`

### Territory and route questions

- Is the relevant Income Tax and Capital Gains Tax route UK-wide for the claimant and tax
  year being modelled?
- Does the person still own an existing asset that became negligible in value while they
  owned it, or has an actual disposal or dissolution already occurred?
- Were shares subscribed for, acquired another way, reorganised or exchanged, and do they
  meet the current qualifying-trading-company conditions?
- Is the proposed treatment a capital-loss notification, a negligible-value deemed
  disposal, share-loss relief against income or a combination requiring separate claims?

### Clock questions

- What conditions must still exist when a negligible-value claim is made?
- How far back may the claimant specify an earlier deemed-disposal time under the current
  legislation?
- What are the separate notification and claim limits for capital losses and share-loss
  relief against income?
- How do dissolution, cessation of trade, a reorganisation or the normal Self Assessment
  filing date affect those limits?

### Authority and professional-gate questions

- What evidence proves ownership, subscription, acquisition cost, prior relief and the
  date on which value allegedly became negligible?
- When must HMRC Shares and Assets Valuation or an independent unquoted-share valuer be
  involved?
- What tax specialism is needed to test EIS, SEIS, reorganisation, connected-person and
  qualifying-company rules?
- Is the work confined to historic tax analysis, and if not, does any recommendation about
  acquiring, retaining or disposing of an investment require FCA authorisation?

### Money, zero and adverse questions

- What is the evidenced allowable loss after acquisition rules and prior relief, and which
  income or gain could lawfully absorb it?
- Which part, if any, remains available for a later period, and how is double use prevented?
- Could the claim fail because the asset did not become negligible, no longer exists, was
  no longer owned or lacks adequate valuation evidence?
- Could prior investment relief reduce the loss, a deferred gain revive, no tax base exist
  to use the loss, or professional valuation cost exceed the tax effect?

### Official and primary entry points

- [Taxation of Chargeable Gains Act 1992](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1992/12/contents)
- [Income Tax Act 2007](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2007/3/contents)
- [HMRC: negligible value claims and agreements](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/negligible-value-agreements)
- [HMRC helpsheet HS286 collection](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/negligible-value-claims-and-income-tax-losses-on-disposals-of-shares-you-have-subscribed-for-in-qualifying-trading-companies-hs286-self-assessment-he)
- [HMRC Venture Capital Schemes Manual: share-loss relief](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/venture-capital-schemes-manual/vcm70000)

**Candidate-specific promotion condition:** a reviewer must prove the deemed-disposal and
share-loss routes separately, include valuation counterevidence and show that the same
loss cannot be counted twice. The public record must not recommend an investment action.

## What this queue does not do

This queue does not invite anyone to submit a matter, contact a professional, make a
claim or challenge a tax authority or public body. It does not say that an authority made an error. A
procedural complexity, rejected claim or adverse decision is not evidence of injustice.
Any future public-body scrutiny record would need its own official evidence, bounded
statement, counterweight, `doesNotProve` field and correction or response route.
