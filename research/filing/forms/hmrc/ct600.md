# CT600: Corporation Tax Return

> **Form Number**: CT600
> **Authority**: HMRC
> **Last Updated**: 2026-02-01

---

## Overview

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Full Name** | Company Tax Return |
| **Purpose** | Report profits, gains, and calculate Corporation Tax |
| **Who Files** | All UK companies, including dormant |
| **Frequency** | Annual (per accounting period) |
| **Method** | Online only (iXBRL accounts) |

---

## Who Must File

### All Companies

| Entity Type | Required? |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Private limited company (Ltd)** | Yes |
| **Public limited company (PLC)** | Yes |
| **Limited by guarantee (CLG)** | Yes |
| **Community Interest Company (CIC)** | Yes |
| **Charitable company** | Yes (claim exemptions) |
| **Dormant company** | Yes (simplified) |

### NOT Required

| Entity Type | Why Not |
|-------------|---------|
| **LLP** | Files SA800 (partnership return) |
| **General partnership** | Files SA800 |
| **Sole trader** | Files SA100 |
| **CIO** | Files CT600 (but often nil) |

## Deadline

### Filing Deadline

**12 months** after accounting period end.

| Accounting Period End | Filing Deadline |
|----------------------|-----------------|
| 31 December 2025 | 31 December 2026 |
| 31 March 2026 | 31 March 2027 |
| 30 June 2026 | 30 June 2027 |

### Payment Deadline

**9 months and 1 day** after accounting period end.

| Accounting Period End | Payment Deadline |
|----------------------|------------------|
| 31 December 2025 | 1 October 2026 |
| 31 March 2026 | 1 January 2027 |
| 30 June 2026 | 1 April 2027 |

### Large Companies (Quarterly Instalments)

If profits exceed £1.5m (divided by associated companies):

| Instalment | Due Date |
|------------|----------|
| 1st | 6 months + 14 days after period start |
| 2nd | 9 months + 14 days after period start |
| 3rd | 14 days after period end |
| 4th | 3 months + 14 days after period end |

### Very Large Companies (>£20m profits)

Instalments based on **current year** estimate (not prior year).

## How to File

### Online Only

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Portal** | HMRC Company Tax Online |
| **Account** | Government Gateway |
| **Format** | iXBRL (inline eXtensible Business Reporting Language) |
| **Accounts** | Must submit tagged accounts in iXBRL |

### Software Required

CT600 must be filed using:
- HMRC's free online service, OR
- Commercial software (Xero, Sage, etc.)

**Paper filing not accepted** (since April 2011).

## What to Include

### CT600 Computations

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Trading profits** | Adjusted accounting profit |
| **Property income** | UK and overseas rental |
| **Loan relationships** | Interest income/expense |
| **Chargeable gains** | Capital disposals |
| **Donations** | Gift Aid deductions |
| **Losses** | Current year, carry back/forward |
| **R&D credits** | SME or RDEC claims |
| **Group relief** | Claims/surrenders |

### iXBRL Accounts

Statutory accounts must be:
- Tagged in iXBRL format
- Submitted with CT600
- Filed at Companies House (separately)

## Penalties

### Late Filing (FA 1998 Sch.18)

| Delay | Penalty |
|-------|---------|
| **1 day late** | £100 |
| **3 months late** | Additional £100 |
| **6 months late** | 10% of unpaid tax |
| **12 months late** | Additional 10% of unpaid tax |

**Repeat offenders**: £500 for first two penalties if late 3 times in succession.

### Late Payment

| Delay | Penalty |
|-------|---------|
| **30 days** | No automatic penalty |
| **Interest** | Bank Rate + 2.5% from due date |

**Note**: CT late payment doesn't have same 5%/5%/5% structure as SA - mainly interest.

### Failure to Keep Records

Up to **£3,000** per accounting period.

---

## Special Regimes

### Charities (CT600E)

- Claim exemptions on trading, property, investment income
- Must show income applied for charitable purposes
- File even if fully exempt

### REITs (Property Income Distributions)

- Ring-fence property business
- Additional reporting on CT600
- Withholding on PIDs

### R&D Tax Relief (CT600L)

- SME scheme: Enhanced deduction
- RDEC: Tax credit
- Detailed project reporting required

---

## Associated Companies

### Effect on Small Profits Rate

The £50,000/£250,000 limits are divided by:
```
1 + Number of associated companies
```

**Associated** = Under common control (51%+)

| Associates | Lower Limit | Upper Limit |
|------------|-------------|-------------|
| 0 | £50,000 | £250,000 |
| 1 | £25,000 | £125,000 |
| 2 | £16,667 | £83,333 |
| 3 | £12,500 | £62,500 |
