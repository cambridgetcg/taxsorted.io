---

## Key Principle

> A CIC is a **limited company** with a **community purpose** but is **NOT a charity**. CICs pay normal Corporation Tax with NO special exemptions.

**Critical Distinction**: Despite being "social enterprise", CICs do NOT get charitable tax treatment.

## Companies Act 2004 Part 2: CICs

### Section 26: Community Interest Companies

```
(1) A company is a "community interest company" if—
    (a) it is a company limited by shares or a company
        limited by guarantee, and
    (b) it satisfies the community interest test and the
        other requirements imposed by or under this Part.
```

### Section 35: Community Interest Test

```
(1) A company satisfies the community interest test if a
    reasonable person might consider that its activities are
    being carried on for the benefit of the community.

(2) An organisation does not satisfy the test if—
    (a) any of its activities is carried on only for the
        benefit of its members, or
    (b) all its activities are political.
```

### Section 36: Excluded Companies (Cannot be CICs)

```
A company is not eligible to be a community interest company if—
(a) it is a charity,
(b) it is a political party.
```

## The Asset Lock

### Section 30: Asset Lock

```
A community interest company must not—
(a) transfer any of its assets other than for full
    consideration, or
(b) on a dissolution or winding up, distribute any
    assets to its members.
```

**Effect**: CIC assets must be used for community benefit, not extracted by shareholders.

### Permitted Transfers

| Transfer To | Permitted? |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Another CIC** | Yes |
| **Charity** | Yes |
| **Prescribed body** | Yes |
| **Members (as dividend)** | Limited (see dividend cap) |
| **Members (on winding up)** | No |

## Dividend Cap

### CIC Regulations 2005, Regulation 17

CICs limited by shares can pay dividends, but with caps:

**Current Caps (from 2014)**:

| Cap Type | Limit |
|----------|-------|
| **Maximum dividend per share** | 20% of paid-up value |
| **Aggregate cap** | 35% of distributable profits |

**Example**:
```
CIC has:
- £100,000 paid-up share capital
- £50,000 distributable profits

Maximum dividend:
- Per share cap: £100,000 × 20% = £20,000
- Aggregate cap: £50,000 × 35% = £17,500

Actual maximum: £17,500 (lower of two)
```

## Tax Treatment: Normal Corporation Tax

### No Special Exemptions

Unlike charities, CICs receive **NO tax exemptions**:

| Tax | CIC Treatment | Charity Treatment |
|-----|--------------|-------------------|
| **Trading profits** | Fully taxable | Exempt (if primary purpose) |
| **Investment income** | Fully taxable | Exempt |
| **Capital gains** | Fully taxable | Exempt |
| **Gift Aid** | Cannot receive | Can receive |

### Corporation Tax Rates (Same as any company)

| Profit Level | Rate (FY 2025) |
|--------------|----------------|
| £0 - £50,000 | 19% |
| £50,001 - £250,000 | 19-25% (marginal) |
| £250,001+ | 25% |

### One Tax Advantage (Ended 2023)

SITR provided relief for investors in social enterprises including CICs:

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|

| **Period** | 2014-2023 |
| **Status** | **EXPIRED** 5 April 2023 |
| **Replacement** | None announced |

**Source**: ITA 2007 Part 5B (now historical)

## Governance Requirements

### CIC Reports

Annual CIC report required (CIC Regulations 2005, reg.26):

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Activities** | Description of community benefit |
| **Stakeholder engagement** | How stakeholders consulted |
| **Directors' remuneration** | Disclosure required |
| **Dividends** | Amounts paid |
| **Asset transfers** | Transfers made |

### CIC Regulator

Overseen by the CIC Regulator (part of Companies House):
- Reviews community interest test
- Monitors compliance
- Can investigate complaints
- Can remove CIC status

## CIC vs Charity Comparison

| Aspect | CIC | Charity |
|--------|-----|---------|
| **Tax on trading** | Full CT | Exempt |
| **Tax on investment** | Full CT | Exempt |
| **Gift Aid** | Cannot receive | Can receive |
| **Asset lock** | Yes | Yes |
| **Dividend cap** | Yes (20%/35%) | Cannot pay dividends |
| **Profit distribution** | Limited | Prohibited |
| **Regulator** | CIC Regulator | Charity Commission |
| **Public benefit test** | Community interest | Charitable purposes |

---

## Why Choose CIC Over Charity?

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Commercial flexibility** | Can trade freely |
| **Can pay dividends** | Attract investors |
| **Simpler regulation** | No Charity Commission |
| **Clearer brand** | "Social enterprise" vs "charity" |
| **Asset lock protection** | Prevents extraction |

### When NOT to Use CIC

If you can qualify as a charity, generally better due to tax exemptions - unless:
- Need to attract equity investors
- Want to pay limited dividends
- Activities don't meet charitable purposes test

## Formation

### Steps to Create CIC

1. Form company (Ltd by shares or guarantee)
2. Include "Community Interest Company" in name
3. Submit CIC36 (community interest statement)
4. Articles must include asset lock provisions
5. CIC Regulator reviews and approves

### Converting Existing Company

- Submit CIC37 form
- Pass special resolution
- Amend articles
- CIC Regulator approval
