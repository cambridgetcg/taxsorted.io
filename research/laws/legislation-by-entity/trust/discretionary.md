## Overview

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Legal Form** | Trust (opaque) |
| **Separate Legal Personality** | No (but taxed as quasi-entity) |
| **Tax Personality** | **Opaque** - trustees taxed |
| **Primary Tax** | Income Tax at trust rates |
| **CGT Applicable** | Yes (on trustees, 24%) |
| **IHT Applicable** | Relevant property regime (periodic/exit charges) |
| **VAT Applicable** | N/A |
| **NI Applicable** | N/A |

---

## Key Principle

> A discretionary trust gives trustees **discretion** over distributions. No beneficiary has a fixed entitlement. For tax, this means the trust is taxed as a **separate entity** at special trust rates.

**Contrast with Bare Trust**: Bare = transparent (beneficiary taxed). Discretionary = opaque (trustees taxed).

## Income Tax: Trust Rates

### ITA 2007 Section 479: The Trust Rate

```
(1) Income tax is charged at the trust rate on so much of—
    (a) the income of the trustees of a settlement, as is
        not within subsection (2).

(2) Income tax is charged at the dividend trust rate on
    so much of that income as is dividend income.
```

### Trust Tax Rates (2025-26)

| Income Type | Rate | Comparison |
|-------------|------|------------|
| **Non-dividend** | 45% (trust rate) | vs 45% additional rate |
| **Dividend** | 39.35% (dividend trust rate) | vs 39.35% additional rate |

**Note**: Trust rates match additional rate taxpayer rates.

### Standard Rate Band (ITA 2007 s.491)

First £1,000 of trust income taxed at basic/dividend ordinary rates:

| Income Type | Standard Band Rate |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Non-dividend** | 20% |
| **Dividend** | 8.75% |

**Divided by settlements**: If settlor creates multiple trusts, £1,000 divided between them.

## Tax Pool and Distributions

### ITA 2007 Section 494-496: Tax Credits on Distribution

When discretionary trust distributes to beneficiary:

```
DISTRIBUTION MECHANISM

1. Trust receives £1,000 income
2. Trustees pay tax at 45% = £450
3. Trust has £550 available
4. Trust distributes £550 to beneficiary
5. Beneficiary receives as "net" amount
6. Grossed up = £1,000 (£550 × 100/55)
7. Beneficiary taxed on £1,000 gross
8. Credit for £450 "tax pool"
```

### Tax Pool

- Trust maintains "pool" of tax paid
- Credit given to beneficiaries on distribution
- If pool exhausted: trust pays additional 45% on distribution

## Capital Gains Tax

### TCGA 1992 Section 69: Trust Rate for CGT

```
The rate of capital gains tax in respect of gains accruing
to the trustees of a settlement shall be determined in
accordance with section 4.
```

### CGT Rate for Trusts (2025-26)

| Asset Type | Rate |
|------------|------|
| **Residential property** | 24% |
| **Other assets** | 24% |

**Compare to individuals**: Basic rate taxpayer pays 10%/18%, higher rate pays 20%/24%.

### Annual Exempt Amount

| Year | Full Amount | Trust Amount |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| 2024-25 | £3,000 | £1,500 |
| 2025-26 | £3,000 | £1,500 |

Trusts get HALF the individual exempt amount.

## IHT: Relevant Property Regime

### IHTA 1984 Section 58: Relevant Property

```
"Relevant property" means settled property in which no
qualifying interest in possession subsists.
```

Discretionary trusts are the classic "relevant property" trust.

### Three Charges

| Charge | When | Rate |
|--------|------|------|
| **Entry charge** | On creation | Up to 20% |
| **Periodic charge** | Every 10 years | Up to 6% |
| **Exit charge** | On distribution | Proportionate to periodic |

### Entry Charge (s.64)

If settlor creates discretionary trust:
- Chargeable lifetime transfer (CLT)
- If >£325,000 nil rate band: 20% on excess
- Becomes 40% if settlor dies within 7 years (less taper relief)

### Periodic Charge (s.64)

Every 10th anniversary of trust creation:
- Calculate notional transfer (trust value + previous CLTs)
- Apply lifetime rate (20%)
- Multiply by 30% (giving max 6% effective rate)
- Apply to trust value

**Effective rate calculation**:
```
Effective Rate = 6% × (Trust Value above NRB / Trust Value) × (Quarters since last charge / 40)
```

### Exit Charge (s.65)

When property leaves trust:
- Proportionate to last periodic charge
- Based on complete quarters since last periodic/entry

## Settlements Code (Anti-Avoidance)

### ITTOIA 2005 Part 5 Chapter 5: Settlements on Settlor's Children

If settlor retains interest or benefits minor child:

| Section | Rule |
|---------|------|
| **s.619** | Definition of "settlement" |
| **s.624** | Income where settlor retains interest |
| **s.629** | Income of minor child of settlor |
| **s.633** | Capital sums paid to settlor |

**Effect**: Income taxed on SETTLOR, not trustees/beneficiaries.

## Pre-2006 vs Post-2006 Trusts

### FA 2006 Changes

| Aspect | Pre-22 March 2006 | Post-22 March 2006 |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **IIP trusts** | Exempt from relevant property | Relevant property (mostly) |
| **Accumulation & Maintenance** | Exempt | Relevant property |
| **Disabled trusts** | Exempt | Still exempt |

**FA 2006** brought most trusts into the "relevant property" regime.

## Common Uses

### Family Wealth Protection

```
DISCRETIONARY TRUST STRUCTURE

Settlor → Creates trust
          │
          ├─ Trustees (3 typically)
          │    ↓
          │    Investment portfolio
          │
          └─ Beneficiaries (class)
              ├─ Spouse
              ├─ Children
              ├─ Grandchildren
              └─ Future issue

Trustees decide who gets what, when.
```

### Advantages

1. **Asset protection**: Assets not in beneficiaries' names
2. **Flexibility**: Trustees adapt to circumstances
3. **IHT planning**: Can skip generations
4. **Vulnerable beneficiaries**: Protect from claims

### Disadvantages

1. **High income tax rates**: 45%
2. **IHT charges**: Entry/periodic/exit
3. **Complexity**: Professional trustees often needed
4. **Cost**: Ongoing administration
