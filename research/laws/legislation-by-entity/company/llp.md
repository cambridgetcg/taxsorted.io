# Limited Liability Partnership (LLP): Legislative Profile

> **Last Updated**: 2026-02-01
> **Confidence**: High
> **Status**: Complete

---

## Overview

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Legal Form** | Incorporated (LLP Act 2000) |
| **Separate Legal Personality** | Yes |
| **Tax Personality** | **Transparent** (default) - members taxed |
| **Primary Tax** | Income Tax on members (or CT if corporate member) |
| **CGT Applicable** | Yes (on members) |
| **IHT Applicable** | On members' interests |
| **VAT Applicable** | Standard rules |
| **NI Applicable** | Class 2 & 4 on individual members |

---

## Key Tax Principle

> **LLPs are tax-transparent**: The LLP itself is not taxed. Each member is taxed on their share of profits as if they were a partner in a traditional partnership.

However, an LLP can be taxed as a company in certain circumstances (see below).

## Tax Treatment: Transparency Rule

### ITTOIA 2005, Section 863: The Transparency Rule

**Text**:
```
For income tax purposes—
(a) all the activities of the LLP are treated as carried on in
    partnership by its members (and not by the LLP as such), and
(b) the property of the LLP is treated as held by the members
    as partnership property.
```

**Effect**:
- LLP = transparent for income tax
- Members taxed on share of profits
- Same rules as general partnerships apply

**Source**: [ITTOIA 2005 s.863](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2005/5/section/863)

---

### CTA 2009, Section 1273: Corporate Members

Where a company is a member of an LLP, the company is charged to Corporation Tax on its share of LLP profits.

```
Member Type → Tax Treatment
─────────────────────────────
Individual    → Income Tax (ITA 2007)
Company       → Corporation Tax (CTA 2009)
Trust         → Trust rates (ITA 2007)
```

## When LLP Is Taxed as a Company

### "Salaried Member" Rules (ITTOIA 2005 ss.863A-863G)

From 6 April 2014, certain LLP members are treated as **employees** not partners.

**Three Conditions (ALL must be met for salaried member treatment)**:

| Condition | Test |
|-----------|------|
| **Condition A** | Member's profit share is "disguised salary" (fixed, not variable with profits) |
| **Condition B** | Member has no significant influence over LLP affairs |
| **Condition C** | Member's capital contribution <25% of "disguised salary" |

**If all conditions met**:
- Member treated as employee
- LLP must operate PAYE
- Employer's NI due on payments

**Source**: FA 2014, introducing ITTOIA 2005 ss.863A-863G

---

### CTA 2010, Section 1273: LLP Taxed as Company

An LLP is taxed as a company (not transparent) if:
1. It carries on a trade or business with a view to profit, AND
2. It elects to be treated as a company, OR
3. It is a "collective investment scheme" LLP

**In practice**: Most trading LLPs remain transparent. Corporate taxation applies mainly to investment LLPs.

## Member Taxation (Transparent LLP)

### Individual Members

Same as sole trader/partner:

| Tax | Treatment |
|-----|-----------|
| **Income Tax** | On share of profits (ITA 2007) |
| **Class 2 NI** | Voluntary (from Apr 2024) |
| **Class 4 NI** | 6% on £12,571-£50,270; 2% above |
| **Self Assessment** | Required (SA104) |

### Corporate Members

| Tax | Treatment |
|-----|-----------|
| **Corporation Tax** | On share of profits (CTA 2009) |
| **CT600** | Include LLP share |

---

## Profit Allocation

### ITA 2007, Section 850: Allocation of Profits

Profits allocated according to LLP agreement (or equally if no agreement).

**Anti-Avoidance**: ITA 2007 ss.850A-850C (mixed membership partnerships) - restricts profit shifting to corporate members to reduce overall tax.

## LLP vs Partnership vs Company Comparison

| Aspect | General Partnership | LLP | Ltd Company |
|--------|-------------------|-----|-------------|
| **Legal Personality** | No (E&W) | Yes | Yes |
| **Limited Liability** | No | Yes | Yes |
| **Tax Transparency** | Yes | Yes (default) | No |
| **Filing** | SA800 | SA800 + LLP accounts | CT600 + accounts |
| **Public Disclosure** | Minimal | Accounts at CH | Accounts at CH |
