## Overview

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Legal Form** | Incorporated (Companies Act 2006) |
| **Separate Legal Personality** | Yes |
| **Tax Personality** | Separate from shareholders/directors |
| **Primary Tax** | Corporation Tax |
| **CGT Applicable** | Yes (corporate gains within CT) |
| **IHT Applicable** | No (but shares in estate may be) |

## Section-by-Section Analysis

### CTA 2010, Section 3: The Main Rate

**Purpose**: Sets the rate at which corporation tax is charged

**Original (1 April 2010)**:
```
Corporation tax is charged at the rate set by Parliament for
the financial year ('the main rate').
```

**Note**: CTA 2010 s.3 doesn't contain the actual rate percentage - it's set each year by Finance Acts and expressed as the "main rate" for each financial year.

### CTA 2010, Part 3: Companies with Small Profits (ss.18-34)

**Purpose**: Provides reduced rate for companies with profits below threshold

**Key Sections**:

| Section | Title | Purpose |
|---------|-------|---------|
| s.18 | Introduction | Framework for small profits rate |
| s.18A | Standard small profits rate | Rate for qualifying companies |
| s.19 | Lower limit | £50,000 (from Apr 2023) |
| s.20 | Upper limit | £250,000 (from Apr 2023) |
| s.24 | Associated companies | Limits divided between associates |

**Marginal Relief Formula (from 1 April 2023)**:

```
Marginal Relief = (Upper Limit - Profits) × (Profits / Profits) × 3/200

Effective Rate = Main Rate - (Marginal Relief / Profits)
```

| Profit Level | Tax Rate | Marginal Rate |
|--------------|----------|---------------|
| £0 - £50,000 | 19% | N/A |
| £50,001 - £250,000 | 19-25% | 26.5% |
| £250,001+ | 25% | N/A |

**Source**: [CTA 2010 Part 3](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2010/4/part/3)

**Purpose**: Rules for calculating trading profits for corporation tax

**Key Sections**:

| Section | Title | Rule |

### CTA 2009, Part 5: Loan Relationships (ss.292-476)

**Purpose**: Taxation of interest and financing costs

**Key Principles**:
- All interest income/expense through loan relationships
- Fair value or amortised cost accounting
- "Fairly represents" test
- No longer "Schedule D Case III" approach

**Key Sections**:

**Source**: [CTA 2009 Part 5](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2009/4/part/5)

### CTA 2010, Part 5: Group Relief (ss.97-188)

**Purpose**: Allows losses to be surrendered between group companies

**Key Sections**:

| Section | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| s.97 | Overview of Part |
| s.99 | Claims for group relief |
| s.130 | Definition of group |
| s.151 | 75% subsidiary requirement |

**Group Definition**:
- Parent owns ≥75% of subsidiary's ordinary share capital
- Parent entitled to ≥75% of distributable profits and assets on winding up

**Source**: [CTA 2010 Part 5](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2010/4/part/5)

## Tax Calculation Summary

### Corporation Tax Calculation (FY 2025)

```
Gross Trading Profits (CTA 2009 Part 3)
     └─ Add: Loan relationship credits (CTA 2009 Part 5)
     └─ Add: Chargeable gains (TCGA 1992)
     └─ Less: Loan relationship debits
     └─ Less: Management expenses
     └─ Less: Qualifying charitable donations
     └─ Less: Loss relief (CTA 2010 Part 4)
= Taxable Total Profits

Apply Rate:
├─ £0 - £50,000:         19%
├─ £50,001 - £250,000:   19-25% (marginal relief)
└─ £250,001+:            25%
```

## Director & Shareholder Taxation

### Director's Salary
- Company deducts as expense (CTA 2009 s.54)
- Director taxed as employment income (ITEPA 2003)
- Employer's NI: 15% above £5,000 (2025-26)

### Dividends
- Company: No deduction (paid from post-tax profits)
- Shareholder: Dividend tax rates

| Dividend Rates (2025-26) | Rate |
|--------------------------|------|
| Allowance | £500 |
| Basic rate | 8.75% |
| Higher rate | 33.75% |
| Additional rate | 39.35% |

**Note**: From April 2026, rates increase to 10.75%, 35.75%, 39.85%

## Close Company Provisions (CTA 2010 Part 10)

Most private companies are "close companies" (controlled by 5 or fewer participators).

**Key Rules**:

| Section | Rule |
|---------|------|
| s.455 | Tax on loans to participators (33.75%) |
| s.459 | Release of loans |
| s.464A | Arrangements to avoid s.455 |

**s.455 Tax**: If company lends to shareholder/director, 33.75% tax charge until repaid.
