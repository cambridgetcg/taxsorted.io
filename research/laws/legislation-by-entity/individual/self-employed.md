## Section-by-Section Analysis

### ITA 2007, Section 35: Personal Allowance

**Purpose**: Sets the amount of income an individual can earn tax-free

**Original (6 April 2007)**:
```
An individual who makes a claim is entitled to a personal allowance of
£5,035 for a tax year if the individual—
(a) is under the age of 65 throughout the tax year, and
(b) meets the requirements of section 56 (residence etc).
```


## Tax Calculation Summary

### Income Tax Calculation (2025-26)

```
Trading Profit (ITTOIA 2005)
     └─ Less: Trading Allowance (£1,000) if applicable
     └─ Less: Pension contributions
     └─ Less: Loss relief
= Net Trading Income

+ Other Income (employment, property, savings, dividends)
= Total Income

- Personal Allowance (£12,570, tapered if >£100k)
= Taxable Income

Tax Calculation:
├─ £0 - £37,700:        20% (basic rate)
├─ £37,701 - £125,140:  40% (higher rate)
└─ £125,141+:           45% (additional rate)
```

### National Insurance Calculation (2025-26)

```
Trading Profit
     │
     ├─ Class 2: Voluntary (£3.50/week if paid)
     │
     └─ Class 4:
        ├─ £0 - £12,570:          0%
        ├─ £12,571 - £50,270:     6%
        └─ £50,271+:              2%
```
